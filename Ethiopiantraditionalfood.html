Ethiopian Traditional Food

Ethiopian most popular food

For this recipe, we are essentially making a thick, meaty tomato sauce and layering that with noodles and cheese into a casserole. Here's the run-down

Bayenetu (mixed vegitable)

  • Injera
  • cabbage
  • Atir kik wot
  • carrot
  • potatoes
  • Tikil gomen
  • Misir wat

Steps

  1. Injera Combine the teff flour and 12 cups water. Mix, cover and store in a dark, cool place for 3 to 4 days to ferment, giving it its sour flavor
  2. Heat a large cast-iron pan over medium heat. Pour 1 cup fermented mixture into it, swirling the pan to cover the entire surface area
  3. Cover with a lid, 2 to 3 minutes. Injera is ready to plate when holes have formed on the surface. Place the injera on a plate and serve.
  4. Heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  5. Cook the carrots and onion in the hot oil about 5 minutes.
  6. Stir in the salt, pepper, cumin, turmeric, and cabbage and cook another 15 to 20 minutes.
  7. Add the potatoes; cover.
  8. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook until potatoes are soft, 20 to 30 minutes.
